Prof. Wole Soyinka

Here's a timeline of Prof. Soyinka's life:

  • 1934 - Akinwande Oluwole Babatunde Soyinka was born into a Yoruba family in Abeokuta, Nigeria.
  • 1954 - He attended Government College in Ibadan and subsequently University College Ibadan and the University of Leeds in England.
  • 1965 - He seized the Western Nigeria Broadcasting Service studio and broadcast a demand for the cancellation of the Western Nigeria Regional Elections. Before then, he has taken an active role in Nigeria's political history and its struggle for independence from Great Britain.
  • 1967 - During the Nigerian Civil War, he was arrested by the federal government of General Yakubu Gowon and put in solitary confinement for two years.
  • 1988 - 1991 - He taught at Cornell University as Goldwin Smith Professor for African Studies and Theatre Arts.
  • 1996 - He was appointed Robert W. Woodruff Professor of the Arts at Emory University
  • 1999 - With the return of democracy to Nigeria, he returned to Nigeria after escaping on a motorcycle via the "NADECO Route." He was made Professor emeritus
  • 2008 - After having been a Professor of Creative Writing at the University of Nevada, Las Vegas, and served as scholar-in-residence at NYU's Institute of African American Affairs and at Loyola Marymount University in Los Angeles, California, US, he was a distinguished Scholar in Residence in 2008.
  • 2017 - He was awarded the Europe Theatre Prize in the "Special Prize" category awarded to someone who has "contributed to the realization of cultural events that promote understanding and the exchange of knowledge between peoples".

"The man dies in him who keeps silent in the face of tyranny" - Prof. Wole Soyinka.
